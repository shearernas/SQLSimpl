Topic 5: Logging into MySQL

MySQL does not work within a regular window, like your web browser or productivity software might.

Instead, you must use the command line interface to log into MySQL and use it.

The "command line interface" (CLI) is like a very barebones way of interacting with your operating system.

It allows you to type in commands that tell your computer directly what it should do.

It might seem super intimidating because it isn't a very "visual" way of interacting with your computer, but just to log into MySQL, it doesn't need much input!

On macOS, you'll want to open up the "Terminal.app" application to access your computer's CLI. This can be found in your Launchpad, located on your Dock at the bottom of your screen, or using Spotlight search by pressing the Command + Spacebar keys, then typing "Terminal.app" in the search bar.
